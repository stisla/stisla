Modal

An overlay dialog for confirmations, focused tasks, and quick captures.

Basic

A trigger opens the modal via data-bs-toggle="modal" and data-bs-target. Inside the dialog: an optional .modal-header with the title, a .modal-body for content, and an optional .modal-footer for actions. The dismiss control is .modal-close, a floating frosted pill that sits inside .modal-content so it works whether or not a header is present.

Send a workspace invite. They'll get an email with a link to join and pick a role on first sign-in.

Sizes

Add .modal-sm, .modal-lg, or .modal-xl on the .modal-dialog to swap widths. Default sits at the middle of the scale. For an edge-bleeding layout use .modal-fullscreen; for a viewport-filling card with breathing room override --bs-modal-width and --bs-modal-margin on the dialog.

Small

Compact dialog for short confirmations.

Default

The default width works for most forms and message dialogs.

Large

Roomier for two-column forms or a primary panel with side details.

Extra large

Maxes out at the widest preset. Use for dense layouts that need more horizontal room than .modal-lg allows.

Almost fullscreen

Fills the viewport but keeps a margin, so the rounded corners and the page behind still read.

Fullscreen

Edge-to-edge with no margin or rounded corners. Useful for immersive workflows on mobile or media viewers on desktop.

Positioning

The dialog sticks to the top by default. Add .modal-dialog-centered to pin it vertically center, or .modal-dialog-bottom to anchor it to the lower edge. .modal-dialog-top is available for templates that toggle position at runtime.

Pinned to top

Reads as a notification surface that drops in from above.

Centered

The standard choice for confirmations and focused tasks.

Pinned to bottom

Reads as a sheet on mobile, an action drawer on desktop.
