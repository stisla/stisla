Shipping a 14px baseline

Why we dropped the body text by 2 pixels and what changed downstream.

The framework used to render body copy at 16px, which is the Bootstrap default. That reads fine on a marketing page but feels heavy in a dashboard where every row counts. We took the baseline to 14px and let the heading scale follow.

What moved

Every component that derives its sizing from $font-size-base shrank by about an eighth. That includes buttons, inputs, badges, and table cells. The grid stayed where it was, so column layouts kept their place.

  • Headings dropped proportionally, since the scale multiplies the base.
  • Form controls held their pixel heights through min-height, so dense forms kept their rhythm.
  • Icons sized in em tracked the new baseline for free.

The line you set as the baseline is the line every screen rounds to.

What stayed

Inter loads at 400, 500, 600, and 700. The display weights live on the same family, so a hero title and a body paragraph share their letterforms. Press ? on any docs page to see the rest of the shortcuts.
